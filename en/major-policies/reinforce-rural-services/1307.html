Enhance Digital Services in Remote Areas

  • Share
    • Facebook
    • Twitter
    • LINE
  • Print
Reliable communication networks are a fundamental human right. To promote the deployment of mobile broadband telecommunication networks in remote regions and advance the development of local applications, the Ministry of Digital Affairs (The moda) continuously improves the fee structures for digital communications resources. In 2024, the Standards for the Usage Fees of Radio Frequency were revised to encourage efficient frequency use and market development, thereby ensuring that overall resources serve the public interest.

By establishing coverage factors for mobile communications in Remote areas, along major transportation routes, and in mountainous areas, The moda has successfully increased the population coverage rate for mobile broadband networks in 779 villages across 87 rural townships (towns, cities, and districts), enhanced connectivity across four major transportation segments, and improved network infrastructure in ten mountainous areas. The moda continues to explore policy incentives to guide public and private sector efforts in optimizing network coverage for underserved areas.

In 2024, The moda introduced a new factor for 4G mobile communication networks in rural areas: achieving a population coverage rate of over 99%. Additionally, to promote efficient frequency use, The moda introduced a designated 5G factor to guide mobile broadband operators in expanding 5G coverage throughout economically unviable areas, including remote regions and major transportation routes such as the Taiwan Railways Eastern Line and the Suhua Highway. These measures aim to ensure inclusive access to digital services for all communities.

To ensure stable and high-quality network access and realize digital rights in Remote areas, The moda actively conducts field inspections of communications coverage in Remote areas, along major transportation segments (including railways and highways), and in mountainous areas. These efforts include On site data connection and voice call tests to verify the improvement of communication quality in Remote areas, along major transportation routes, and in mountainous areas. This initiative reflects an ongoing commitment to bridging the digital divide between urban and Remote areas.

As the driving force behind the development of Taiwan's digital industry, The moda continues to promote the Review Mechanism for Discounted Frequency Usage Fees for Telecom Operators Participating in Digital Diverse Application Services. This initiative encourages telecom operators to collaborate across sectors to offer innovative application services, including vertical applications, digital trust, data innovation, and energy sustainability. By developing novel 5G application models, The moda aims to enhance Taiwan's digital competitiveness and strengthen the accessibility and applications of digital technologies.

Innovative Applications Event Highlights

The Ministry of Digital Affairs continues to encourage telecommunications operators to promote diverse digital application services, including vertical applications, digital trust, data innovation, and energy-efficient sustainability, to facilitate efficient frequency usage and ensure that overall resources align with public interests.

Vertical Application Services: Expanding 5G vertical applications in the telecom sector
Collaborating with specific field stakeholders and utilizing auction-acquired radio frequencies to provide vertical application services for key domains. These include applications such as smart disaster prevention, smart education, smart healthcare, and smart transportation, aimed at advancing 5G vertical applications and promoting digital technology adoption in Remote areas.

Digital Trust Services: Establishing a trusted telecommunications usage environment
Applicants can develop their own internal systems or collaborate externally to create applications and services that establish a secure and trusted telecom environment, contribute to social welfare, enhance people's daily lives, or provide improved communication experiences with measurable benefits.

Providing cross-domain analytical services to the public sector using telecommunications signaling data
Leveraging telecom signaling statistics to offer cross-domain analysis services to governmental agencies and public enterprises, covering areas such as traffic flow analysis and crowd movement predictions.

Promotion of Energy Conservation and Sustainability: Deploying high-efficiency, energy-saving mobile network equipment
Enhancing frequency utilization efficiency while emphasizing energy conservation, enabling citizens to enjoy the results of telecommunications infrastructure development while demonstrating concrete electricity-saving impacts that support environmental sustainability.

Given that radio frequency is a scarce public resource, Taiwan adopts a reasonable frequency fee mechanism to enhance the efficient use of spectrum resources. These fee standards are reviewed and adjusted annually based on operators' performance; service penetration; total allocated bandwidth; and the maturity of related technologies, markets, and services.

To promote universal digital access, The moda continuously evaluates radio signal coverage and the difficulty of base station installation throughout Taiwan. By coordinating digital resources across various agencies and working in partnership with telecom operators, The moda is actively expanding 5G infrastructure, fostering diverse cross-sector 5G applications, and driving innovative services. These efforts aim to accelerate Taiwan's digital economy and achieve balanced digital development nationwide.
